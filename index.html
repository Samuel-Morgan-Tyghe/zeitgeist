🧭zeitgeist

What's real, what's overhyped, and what's next in AI engineering — distilled twice daily.

Updated 2026-06-20 · AM
In plain English

For a year the AI conversation has been about making computers more independent — programs that "do things for you" on their own. The most respected people in the field are now quietly saying that's not the real problem any more. The hard part is knowing whether the computer actually did the job — and remembering what it did between Tuesday and Wednesday.

Read on if you want the named ideas. Stop here and you've still got the headline.

01 ·What people used to think → what's changed

Was More autonomous "AI agents" = more capable. Just keep scaling them up.
→
Now More autonomy = more brittle. The lever is evals (rigorously testing what the AI produced) and verifiability (can you prove it was right?).
Was The next breakthrough will come from a smarter model.
→
Now The next battleground is where AIs live and what they remember — "runtime" and persistence. OpenAI just bought Ona for exactly this.
Was "Agentic" meant cutting-edge.
→
Now "Agentic" means almost nothing. Five engineers give five different definitions. When everyone says a word, the word is dead.
Was Write documentation; train teams to read it.
→
Now Write "skills" — small executable instructions an AI can follow. "No one wanted to write docs, but everyone is writing skills."

02 ·Today's TL;DR the named version

Most important

Hamel Husain, Shreya Shankar & Andrej Karpathy reject "agentic" hype for evals + verifiability. OpenAI buying Ona says the next battleground is agent runtime/persistence, not smarter models.

Most overhyped

"Agentic" — five engineers, five answers. "Loops" is at peak too: Steinberger's post hit ~6.5M views — peak velocity is the peak signal.

Emerging

Ontology (tempered — real but slow-burn), dark factories (code shipped with zero human review), docs → skills.
